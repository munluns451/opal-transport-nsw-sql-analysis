# Opal Transport NSW Analysis 🚆🚌⛴️

## Project Overview
Analysis of real Sydney public transport data from Transport for NSW 
Open Data Hub using SQL and SQLite. The dataset contains 5,589 records 
of monthly Opal card trip counts across all transport modes from 2016 to 2025.

## Data Source
Official government data from Transport for NSW Open Data Hub
https://opendata.transport.nsw.gov.au

## Tools Used
- SQL (SQLite)
- DB Browser for SQLite

## Dataset
- 5,589 rows of real NSW government transport data
- Transport modes: Train, Bus, Ferry, Light Rail, Metro
- Date range: July 2016 to July 2025
- Card types: Adult, Concession, Senior, Student and more

## Business Questions & Key Findings
1. Train is most used mode with 2.38 billion trips across all years
2. Pre-COVID peak was 2019 at 753M trips — COVID caused 56% crash in 2021
3. Adult Opal card accounts for 2.7 billion trips — 56% of all trips
4. Ferry was hit hardest by COVID with 65% drop due to loss of tourists
5. March has highest trips as Sydney returns to work after summer holidays
6. Train and Bus together = 93% of all Sydney public transport trips

## Recommendations for Transport for NSW

1. Invest more in Train capacity
   Train carries 49.5% of all trips. More trains and longer 
   carriages needed especially during March peak period.

2. Accelerate Metro expansion
   Metro is only 1.8% of trips but is the newest and fastest 
   growing mode. Expanding Metro lines will reduce pressure 
   on overcrowded train network.

3. Rebuild Ferry tourism strategy
   Ferry lost 65% of trips during COVID and has not fully 
   recovered. Partner with tourism operators to promote 
   harbour experiences and attract international visitors back.

4. January off-peak incentives
   January has the lowest trips of any month. Introduce 
   discounted Opal fares in January to encourage travel 
   during holiday period and boost revenue.

5. Target Senior/Pensioner growth
   Senior/Pensioner is 3rd highest card type with 625M trips.
   This demographic is growing in Sydney. Ensure accessibility 
   and comfort features are prioritised on all transport modes.

## Key Insight
COVID caused a catastrophic 56% drop in public transport usage in 2021 
due to Sydney lockdowns. Ferry was hit hardest losing 65% of trips as 
tourist areas emptied. Recovery began in 2022 with 2023 reaching 598M 
trips — strong but still 20% below pre-COVID 2019 levels.
